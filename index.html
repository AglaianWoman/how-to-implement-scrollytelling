Scrollytelling is the best (or the worst depending on your viewpoint). We mostly love it here at The Pudding. But creating a scroll-driven story is hardly a standardize practice, and there are many libraries out there that can help get you there.

In this post, I demonstrate how to tackle a simple scroll-driven chart using six different libraries, and discuss the pros and cons of each implementation. It is inspired by Lisa Charlotte Rost's great post about how to make the same chart using 12 different charting libraries.

I'm not going to get into the different types of scrollytelling or debate the ethics and morality of it. Read these posts for scrollytelling 101.

We are going to use the scroll-to-trigger pattern, where each new "trigger" element (in this case a block of text) tells the chart to update to a new state.
